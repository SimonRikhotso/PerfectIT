Academic and professional experience

Helping Students Turn Confusion Into Confidence

PerfectIT was founded by Simon Rikhotso, a software developer and programming lecturer with more than a decade of experience teaching programming in academic environments.

Working in both education and software development makes it possible to connect the theory students meet in class with the practical thinking required to design, write and improve real programs.

Every lesson is built around one goal: helping the student understand the reasoning behind the code so they can solve the next problem with greater independence.

What guides the teaching

The PerfectIT Learning Philosophy

Strong programmers are developed through understanding, practice and constructive feedback.

01

Understand First

Complex ideas are broken into clear steps before students are expected to apply them.

02

Practise With Purpose

Guided exercises develop problem-solving ability instead of encouraging copied solutions.

03

Build Independence

Support gradually shifts toward independent thinking, debugging and confident decision-making.

04

Apply the Knowledge

Projects and realistic programming problems turn theory into skills students can demonstrate.

A structured learning journey

From “I Don’t Understand” to “I Can Build This”

Students do not all arrive at the same level. The learning process meets them where they are, strengthens missing foundations and then develops their ability step by step.
