PERFECTIT HOME ADVERT — PRODUCTION GUIDE
========================================

PURPOSE
-------
Create a 36-second brand advert combining:
- a calm voice-over;
- very short clips of Simon teaching;
- natural teaching audio between narration lines;
- programming screens, student projects and class activity;
- a polished PerfectIT ending.

The website expects one final exported file:

videos/home-advert/perfectit-home-advert.mp4

The individual clips remain modular inside videos/home-advert/clips/ so they can
be replaced during future edits.

--------------------

Voice-over: “Every great software developer started exactly where you are.”

CLIP RULES
----------
1. Keep each teaching clip between 2 and 4 seconds.
2. Select teaching audio that makes sense without the full lesson.
3. Avoid showing private student information, marks, email addresses or class lists.
4. Crop screens so code is visible but confidential details are not.
5. Use landscape 16:9 footage whenever possible.
6. Avoid more than two transition styles. Clean cuts and short fades are enough.
7. Keep on-screen text short and large enough for mobile viewers.
8. Add subtitles for both narration and Simon’s teaching audio.
9. Keep background music below all speech.
10. Make the final logo fade connect naturally to the opening so the loop feels smooth.


AUDIO LEVEL STARTING POINTS
---------------------------
Narration: approximately -6 dB to -3 dB peak.
Teaching speech: approximately -6 dB to -3 dB peak.
Background music beneath speech: approximately -28 dB to -20 dB.
Music in gaps without speech: approximately -18 dB to -14 dB.

Adjust by listening; clear speech is more important than exact numbers.


HOW TO ACTIVATE THE VIDEO
-------------------------
1. Export the final advert as perfectit-home-advert.mp4.
2. Copy it to videos/home-advert/.
3. Optionally export a poster image as poster.jpg into the same folder.
4. Open data/videos.json.
5. Find home-brand-advert.
6. Change "enabled": false to "enabled": true.
7. Test through a local server, not by double-clicking the HTML file.

The video loops automatically. It begins muted because modern browsers block
unsolicited audio. A visitor can choose “Play with sound,” after which the full
narration and teaching audio play and repeat.
